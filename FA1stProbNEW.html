Probation Outcome Descriptive Summaries

Population includes a total of 46,905 First-time freshmen from entering cohort 2010 through 2015. Proabtion at 1st Fall is defined by the academic status at the end of the 1st Fall term with either one of PROB/FINL/RECS/DISM. The overall probation rate at 1st fall varies from 8.4% to 10.4% by cohorts.

Finanical Aid Variables Summaries

The logic to pull Financial Aid data is provided by Office of Financial Aid. The Data was pull on 1-12-2017. The loan and cost amounts reflect the sum of the students finanical aid within the timeframe without breaking down by student level or degrees (E.g for student working on mutliple levels/degrees who entered at 2014, it reflect the sum of the FA amount within FS14,SS15,US15 for working on all levels including levels other than undergraduates).

  • Net Price: Net Price = Cost of attendence - Expected Family Contribution (EFC) - total Gift Aid paid. We think this is close to a representation of students finanical burden. For students who did not apply for aid, we dont have a net price estimate on them. If students EFC or total Gift Aid paid amount exceeds their cost of attendence, the amount of Net Price is negative. The net price data is higly left skewed. There is not much differences with regard to its distribution among predict GPA groups. Descriptively, probation population has higher amount of net price compared to non-probation population, which is true across all predict GPA groups.
